Student Goals / Outcomes

A SailFuture Academy graduate is equipped with the academic, vocational, and emotional abilities to transition into adulthood and successfully navigate the workforce, personal relationships, and household affairs.

Students begin their journey at the SailFuture Academy in the ninth grade, a critical year to ensure vital, foundational knowledge is acquired. As students progress through grades 9-12, skills and concepts from the previous year are reinforced and expanded upon.

At the conclusion of a studentâ€™s time with SailFuture Academy, we guarantee placement in either a career or in a post-secondary institution. Additionally students leave with a comprehensive digital portfolio that details a curated body of work that represents their knowledge as well as provides a succinct inventory of acquired skills and certifications.

Goals for Graduates

At the conclusion of their time at SailFuture Academy, graduates possess the ability to think critically, speak effectively, and confidently approach problems with a clear understanding of their own strengths and areas of opportunity. Each graduate is a self-aware, independent thinker with the ability to work both independently and in teams in order to enter into adulthood as a responsible citizen with the academic and social emotional tools to achieve financial freedom.

A SailFuture Graduate will be able to:

  • Read and write at or above grade level at the time of matriculation.

  • Evaluate and accurately compute multi-step, multi-variable practical mathematical computations involving geometric principles in order to measure, plan, and completely renovate a residential bathroom.

  • Analyze primary sources of information to a craft viable evidence-based argument about a social issue in the form of a 10 minute speech.

  • Accurately synthesize and implement electrical theory to rewire an electrical panel in a boat.

  • Curate and execute a nutritious, affordable and profitable 5-day 3-course meal service.

  • Design, craft and propose a comprehensive business plan including budgetary components, market research, financial viability, and detailed business model to a panel of the SailFuture business council and members of the St. Petersburg community.

  • Speak intelligently and succinctly about their strengths and weaknesses, their communication preferences, and their curated skill and knowledge acquisition as supported by their Google Site Portfolio.

Student 4-Year Journey

The 9th-grade year is critical to establish culture and trust, assess competency and develop individual learning needs, and to provide students with exposure and foundational skills for future learning in business, culinary, construction, and maritime coursework.

The data for our target student tells us that most of the students will join us well behind grade level in many subject areas. We anticipate significant differentiation for students within a classroom space, and, therefore, will activate support services and intervention specialists in reading, writing, mathematics, and English language.

ELA and STEM coursework is anchored into the theme of business ownership and all coursework is integrated into the blocks and all-day lab experiences in the SailFuture social enterprises.

Character Education

Character education is an integral part of the daily curriculum. SailFuture Academy will identify and focus on core values that our students will exhibit at school, at home, in the community, and in all their work.

All students will participate in maritime-based training to teach and shape character throughout their journey at the SailFuture Academy. Whether on a 2-person racing dinghy or a 30-crew tall ship, sailing commands the very best of every crewmember. It requires trust, teamwork, independence, and problem solving with limited resources. It is on this principle that we base our character education.

SailFuture believes that the process of being educated is not only the process of acquiring knowledge. It is also about belonging and living within a community â€“ for schools are, together with the family, one of the principal means by which young adults grow, develop, and define their personal character. Our crew structure helps to reinforce the idea of family, and through all-day learning labs character is tested and built.

In keeping with our mission to bridge the gap between education and career, our curriculum and crew meeting times provide ample opportunities to have direct conversations about social emotional skills, interpersonal relationships, active listening, and creative problem solving in order to effectively resolve conflicts. Similarly, students receive daily feedback with regard to their time management, maturity, and effort. Thus, enabling targeted, individualized conversations with students to address specific, quantitative and qualitative measures of character development and presentation with the learning spaces.
