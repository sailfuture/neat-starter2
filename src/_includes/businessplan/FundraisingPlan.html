Fundraising Plan

We believe in developing a groundbreaking academic model and school business model that prepares students to graduate into business owners and operators. Our academic model is centered around high-quality academics integrated into a thematic framework that helps students apply their education in everyday life.

The development of such a program is not fully sustainable on the public funding available for student vouchers. As such, our business model integrates three Social Enterprises into the school model to ensure financial stability for the school in perpetuity while providing core academic experiences to our students.

In Years 1 - 4, the Academy will run an operational deficit that is supported by SailFuture, Inc, SailFuture’s Social Enterprises, and the Head of School’s private fundraising efforts. The total amount of private support needed in Years 1 - 4 is $2.1M. By Year 5, SailFuture’s earned income from Social Enterprises will total $800,000 per year, which will cover 100% of the school’s operating deficit and ensure the school’s long-term financial sustainability.

Earned Income through Social Enterprises

In addition to providing students with immediate, paid-opportunities to learn all aspects of a business, SailFuture’s social enterprises offer products and services to paying customers that generate income to offset the cost of attendance per student.

Construction Unit

The Construction SEU has a signed $900,000 contract to complete the renovation of the 2nd floor of the school building and the kitchen and restaurant.

The Construction SEU projects $273,050 in net income for year 1.

Culinary Unit

Students will design and build the culinary program during Year 1. The Culinary Social Enterprise will Launch in Year 2.

Revenues for the culinary unit will be derived from catering, meal subscription, and events.

SailFuture Yacht Services

The Maritime SEU will operate a 105’ motor yacht for charter in Florida in the Bahamas. The vessel is currently owned by SailFuture with no debt and is valued at $750,000. The Maritime SEU projects a small deficit in the first 3 years prior to selling the vessel for a net profit of $750,000 in Year 4.

Shared Facility Space

SailFuture’s mental health and case management teams will utilize the school facility for office and training space. Shared services are expected to generate $50,000 in revenues in the first year and all years following.

Step Up For Students, FTC, and McKay Scholarships

SailFuture will receive $6,550 per student per year in scholarship funding through low income scholarships and $13,550 per student per year from students receiving McKay scholarships.

Private Philanthropy

SailFuture has a strong track record of private fundraising and plans to expand outreach and efforts to secure additional resources to ensure the Academy’s success. The organization has already raised $2.1M to purchase and renovate the SailFuture Academy’s facility. Fundraising efforts for Year 1 will be dedicated to finishing the capital campaign for the school.

State and National Grants

SailFuture has secured more than $1.5M in state and national grants to date. We plan to engage national funders in the education sector, such as the Walton Family Foundation, to support this innovative school model.

Leadership fundraising track record

SailFuture has raised over $6M in capital and private funding since its founding in 2013 from funders like LinkedIn, Google, TeeKay shipping, LUSH Cosmetics, the state of Florida, and AT&T.
