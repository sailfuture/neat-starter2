Earned Income through
Social Enterprises

In addition to providing students with immediate, paid-opportunities to learn all aspects of a business, SailFuture’s social enterprises offer products and services to paying customers that generate income to offset the cost of attendance per student.

During Year 1, the Social Enterprises Director will launch the Maritime and Construction units. The Culinary Unit will launch in Year 2.

Construction Unit

The Construction SEU has a signed $900,000 contract to complete the renovation of the 2nd floor of the school building and the kitchen and restaurant.

The Construction SEU projects $273,050 in net income for year 1.

Culinary Unit

The Construction SEU has a signed $900,000 contract to complete the renovation of the 2nd floor of the school building and the kitchen and restaurant.

The Construction SEU projects $273,050 in net income for year 1.

Maritime Unit

The Maritime SEU will operate a 105’ motor yacht for charter in Florida in the Bahamas. A projected $125,000 in charter revenues will offset the $200,000 in annual holding costs.

The vessel is currently owned by SailFuture with no debt and is valued at $750,000. The Maritime SEU projects a small deficit in the first 3 years prior to selling the vessel for a net profit of $750,000 in Year 4.

Shared Facility Space

SailFuture’s mental health and case management teams will utilize the school facility for office and training space. Shared services are expected to generate $50,000 in revenues in the first year and all years following.

Step Up For Students, FTC, and McKay Scholarships

SailFuture will receive $6,550 per student per year in scholarship funding through low income scholarships and $13,550 per student per year from students receiving McKay scholarships.

Private philanthropy

SailFuture has a strong track record of private fundraising and plans to expand outreach and efforts to secure additional resources to ensure the Academy’s success.

The organization has already raised $2.1M to purchase and renovate the SailFuture Academy’s facility. Fundraising efforts for Year 1 will be dedicated to finishing the capital campaign for the school.

State and National Grants

Given the highly unique nature of the SailFuture Academy model, we hope to attract funding from state and national funding organizations to support this new educational model.

The SailFuture Academy will also benefit from SailFuture’s breadth and scale, which will allow us to apply for grants in the mental health, education, vocational training, foster care, and juvenile justice areas of interest.
