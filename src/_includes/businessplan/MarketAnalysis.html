Market Analysis

St. Petersburg is a densely populated city that is challenged by consistent growth and economic inequality.

Pinellas County is the 8th largest school district in the state of Florida, serving 104,000 students in the 2019-20 school year.

54,500 students are categorized as economically disadvantaged as determined by free lunch eligibility, and 15,150 students receive exceptional education services as students with disabilities.

The SailFuture Academy is centrally located at the crossroads of 22nd St. N. and I-275, a major thoroughfare within the Pinellas County peninsula.

The school is not housed within a residential zone, but it is within 8-minutes from each of the neighborhoods where our target families reside.

Public School Options

Although Pinellas County Schools has a robust system of programmatic academies and centers housed within its high schools, the schools have failed to equitably engage and serve economically disadvantaged students and their families, as demonstrated by the data above and other measures.

Private School Options

Although some students attend private schools such as Admiral Farragut, Shorecrest, Canterbury, and St. Pete Catholic, these schools are not designed to serve our target population and acceptance is extremely limited.

Charter School Options

MycroSchool (3.2 miles) and NorthStar Academy are public charter schools with a collective enrollment of 391 students. Both schools serve at-risk youth in our target neighborhoods. Their school models are both based on online credit-recovery and are often utilized as an alternative school to divert behaviorally defiant or truant students from the local public schools.

Uniqueness / Selling Factor

SailFuture is the only project-based, private high school focused on providing an equitable and targeted education to economically disadvantaged students in Pinellas County. Our most similar peer, Academy Prep, serves students in grades 5th - 8th.

Parents and students alike are frustrated with the educational experiences provided to students at the neighborhood public high schools (Gibbs, Lakewood, Largo, and Pinellas Park) and perceive NorthStar and MycroSchool as last-resort alternatives.

Evidence of interest in the school from on-the-ground interviews

As of March 31st, 2021, 40% of our Year 1 enrollment projections have been met with minimal marketing. Community partners, parents, and middle school leaders, and District staff have all shown a keen interest and genuine excitement for the launch of the SailFuture Academy.

SailFuture will continue to meet with families and partners over the summer to enroll an additional 30 students before August 2021.
