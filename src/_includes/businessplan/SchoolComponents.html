    Construction Unit

    The Construction SEU has a signed $900,000 contract to complete the renovation of the 2nd floor of the school building and the kitchen and restaurant. The Construction SEU projects $273,050 in net income for year 1.

    Future revenues for the Construction Unit will be a combination of supporting SailFuture’s growth (i.e. the construction of independent living homes) and contracts sourced through the business council.

    All Grades

    Maritime Unit

    The Maritime SEU will operate a 105’ motor yacht for charter in Florida in the Bahamas. A projected $125,000 in charter revenues will offset the $200,000 in annual holding costs.

    The vessel is currently owned by SailFuture with no debt and is currently valued at $750,000. The Maritime SEU projects a small deficit in the first 3 years prior to selling the vessel for a net profit of $625,000 in Year 4.

    Future revenues and projects for the Maritime SEU are built on projected boat donations. The Maritime SEU is expected to solicit 1 major yacht donation per year.

    School Supports

    Non-academic systems of support available to each SailFuture student.

    Crew System

    Crew meetings provide the opportunity for focused character development activities and create a sense of place and belonging. Each student is placed in a crew with the same adult crew leader for the duration of their 4 years Students travel through classes in their crews and conclude each day with a crew meeting (except lab days)

    Mental Health Counseling

    Students have daily access to fully integrated, on-site mental health counseling from a licensed mental health professional specializing in adolescent and trauma-informed strategies.

    Foster Care Case Management

    Students involved in or at-risk of becoming involved in the foster care system will receive integrated foster care case management services from a co-located SailFuture division.

    Mental Health Intervention Specialists

    Mental health first aid-trained support staff help students navigate personal challenges and assist with classroom behavior management.

    Designated 12th grade transition facilitator

    Oversees student transition to next step, coordinates job/college placement, responsible for meeting with each student to help map individualized tasks and needs. Additional touchpoint for parents/guardians for student transition

    Student Feedback Systems

    Daily, weekly, and term-end methods of providing feedback to students and their families.

    Daily Feedback

    Professionalism Scores based on maturity, effort, and time management.

  • Separates behavior and engagement from academic performance
  • Provides real-time feedback to parents to create a stronger partnerships

    • Weekly Feedback

    Students received weekly narrative feedback from each instructor

  • Details academic components such as knowledge/skill mastery, newly emerging mastery/skills, areas of continued growth
  • Provides opportunity to develop a deeper relationship between student and instructor
  • Focuses student personal evaluation on actionable objectives rather than subjective number or letter

    • Term Evaluation

    Students receive an updated skills profile,and receive narrative evaluation from their crew leader as well as from each instructor detailing the student’s presence each term

  • All grade-level faculty meets to discuss each student
  • Determine, based on term’s objectives, which skills a student has increased mastery

    • Benchmark Assessment: MAP

    Utilized at the beginning (Term 1), middle (Term 3), and end of each year (End of Term 5)

  • Utilized to evaluate student content acquisition against national benchmarks and standards
  • Results analyzed in conjunction with internal data (daily, weekly, term) to assess individual student needs and instructional processes

    • Formative and Summative Assessments by class

    Explicit conversation about evaluation rubric at prior to each assessment

  • Quantitative data is collected on the backend by instructors - this is not shared with the students but is used to inform narrative feedback to each student.
  • Narrative evaluation shared with student

    • Transcript & Certifications

    Professionalism score and predictive index

  • Crew leader endorsement and student statement
  • Skills Graph and knowledge acquisition bars
  • Certifications (industry-specific: culinary, construction, maritime)
