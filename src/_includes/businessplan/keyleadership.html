Key Leadership

Our leadership team is

  • Michael Long

    Chief Executive Officer

    Mike is a 200-ton USCG Master Captain and has ten years of experience in building and designing effective programs for at-risk youth. Since 2013, he has raised $6M in capital to advance SailFuture’s mission and has grown the organization to serve 200 youth a year with 35 full time staff. He will assist the school leadership team with operations and play a critical role in the integration of the social enterprises into the academic model.

  • Hunter Thompson

    Operations Director

    Hunter Thompson joined SailFuture as a co-founder in 2014 and is a graduate of Ringling College of Art and Design. He oversees all of SailFuture’s child welfare operations and has extensive experience building SailFuture’s home-school education model from 2018 to present.

  • Lindsay Rapuano

    Head of School

    Lindsay Danielson Rapuano has served as Dean of Students at Ransom Everglades School in Coconut Grove, FL, founding program director at Level The Field, Inc. in New York City, and has taught at various public middle and high schools. Danielson Rapuano has worked closely with students to both create and implement equitable accountability structures and character education curriculum to provide support for students behaviorally as well as emotionally. She earned her BA from Columbia University in the City of New York and MA in Secondary English Education at Teachers College, Columbia University.

  • Samuel Carew-Jones

    Director of Social Enterprises

    Sam is an experienced business leader and educator. He holds a Masters of Arts in teaching from Brandman University and spent most of his teaching career educating adjudicated youth in California. Sam is also an experienced tradesman, holding certificates in Marine Electronics (ABYC ASE L-1, C-1, and A-9), Automotive Technology (ASE Master Technician), and has significant construction experience. Sam was most recently the Direct of IT services for a global portfolio of companies, where he served as the CEO’s “solution’s architect” for all things IT and construction.
