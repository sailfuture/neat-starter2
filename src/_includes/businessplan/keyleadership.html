School Description & Overview

Prepare students to think critically and effectively solve problems in order to become business owners and operators.

  • Andrew Gould

    Board Chair

    Mr. Gould is the co-founder of Presence, a $30M education software business that helps colleges and universities track and measure student engagement. He brings expertise in technology, fundraising, and marketing

  • Dr. Ted Gillette

    Education Committee Chair

    Dr. Gillette is an optometrist, Montessori school leader, and retired CEO of a publicly-traded eyecare company. In his business career he took an eyecare company public and led a national network of optometry clinics. In retirement he took over the middle school his children were attending when it faced a financial crisis. He brings significant experience in business, education, fundraising, and curriculum development.

  • Michael Long

    Chief Executive Officer

    Mike is a 200-ton USCG Master Captain and has ten years of experience in building and designing effective programs for at-risk youth. Since 2013, he has raised $6M in capital to advance SailFuture’s mission and has grown the organization to serve 200 youth a year with 35 full time staff. He will assist the school leadership team with operations and play a critical role in the integration of the social enterprises into the academic model.

  • Hunter Thompson

    Operations Director

    Hunter Thompson joined SailFuture as a co-founder in 2014 and is a graduate of Ringling College of Art and Design. He oversees all of SailFuture’s child welfare operations and has extensive experience building SailFuture’s home-school education model from 2018 to present.

  • Lindsay Rapuano

    Head of School

    Lindsay Danielson Rapuano arrives to SailFuture from Ransom Everglades School in Coconut Grove, FL. As Dean of Students, Danielson Rapuano worked closely with students to both implement equitable accountability structures as well as provide support for students behaviorally as well as emotionally. Danielson Rapuano served as founding program director at Level the Field, Inc., in New York City, where she developed and implemented the inner-city mentorship program that both taught lessons during the school day as well provided weekend engagement opportunities that increased college awareness. She earned her BA from Columbia University in the City of New York and MA in secondary English education at Teachers College, Columbia University.

  • Samuel Carew-Jones

    Director of Social Enterprises

    Sam is an experienced business leader and educator. He holds a Masters of Arts in teaching from Brandman University and spent most of his teaching career educating adjudicated youth in California.

    Sam is also an experienced tradesman, holding certificates in Marine Electronics (ABYC ASE L-1, C-1, and A-9), Automotive Technology (ASE Master Technician), and has significant construction experience.

    Sam was most recently the Direct of IT services for a global portfolio of companies, where he served as the CEO’s “solution’s architect” for all things IT and construction.
