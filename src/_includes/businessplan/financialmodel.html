Financial Model

Our academic model is centered around high-quality academics integrated into a thematic framework that helps students apply their education in everyday life.

The development of such a program is not fully sustainable on the public funding available for student vouchers. As such, our business model integrates three Social Enterprises into the school model to ensure financial stability for the school in perpetuity while providing core academic experiences to our students.

In Years 1 - 4, the Academy will run a significant operational deficit that is supported by SailFuture, Inc and the Head of School’s private fundraising efforts.

The total amount of private support needed in Years 1 - 4 is $2.1M, which includes a $500,000 request from the Drexel Fund ($2,600 per student). The request proposes $200,000 in Year 1, $200,000 in Year 2, and $50,000 in Years 3 and 4.

Support from the Drexel Fund is essential to ensuring a successful launch of the school and helping the organization reach a critical juncture. By Year 5, SailFuture’s earned income will total $800,000 per year, which will cover 100% of the school’s operating deficit.
