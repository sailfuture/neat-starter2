Summer 2022

A Dream Come True: 100' School Ship Donated

For the past 8 years students have lived in small bunks on a 65' long and 12' wide rocket ship of a sailboat. That all changes with the donation of SV Wisdom - a 100' long and 24' sailing ketch with 8 cabins, 6 heads, a classroom, and an onboard dive school.

Fall 2021

We Started A High School -- the SailFuture Academy

We welcomed 50 students to a life-changing 4-year journey in August of 2021. Paid internships, hands-on projects, international sailing expeditions, and a world-class entrepreneurship curriculum prepare students for life and career.

Fall 2022

Major Facility Improvements - Waterfront House Seawall and 30,000sq/ft School Restored

Students helped renovate 30,000 sq/ft of office and classroom space at the 99 year old home of the new school. A major donor stepped up to fund a new seawall at the waterfront house and the Associated Builders and Contractors of the Gulf Coast brought 50 volunteers to renovate the front and backyard with a new retaining wall, concrete pads, and landscaping.

Fall 2022

A social Enterprise was born, and earned $1M in revenue in Year 1

We launched Impact Social Ventures, a specialty marine and residential construction firm in January of 2021. We employed more than 30 students and generated $1M in revenue in our first year. All net income is donated back to SailFuture to fund scholarships for students at the SF Academy.

Summer 2022

105' Motor Yacht Ready for Charter

Student apprentices and graduates transformed a 1955 motor yacht into a state of the art charter vessel. Look out for invitations to book the vessel for private events and vacations throughout 2023.

2023 Initiatives

New Major Initiatives: Culinary Program + Vocational Building + Entrepreneurship Curriculum

$750,000 campaign to renovate an existing 2,500sq/ft commercial kitchen at the SailFuture Academy into a state of the art teaching kitchen and social enterprise.

$1.75M campaign to build a new vocational building at the SF Academy Campus for automotive, carpentry, small engine, and marine paint programs.

$1M campaign to develop open source 4-year entrepreneurship curriculum designed specifically for at-risk students.
