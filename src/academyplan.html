SailFuture

SailFuture Academy

Bridging the gap between high school graduation and a career

Mission Statement

A private entrepreneurship high school that teaches students how to solve real-world problems by practically applying the skills and knowledge acquired through dynamic classroom activities in real-world career settings.

Vision Statement

A private entrepreneurship high school that teaches students how to solve real-world problems by practically applying the skills and knowledge acquired through dynamic classroom activities in real-world career settings.

Student Body

The SailFuture Academy will serve a diverse student population of 14 - 20 year old youth who have disengaged/are on the verge of disengaging from school. SailFuture Academy students are those students needing a kinesthetic learning setting with explicit real-world application and will hail from low-income families eligible for StepUp for Students scholarships, students in foster care, and paying families.

Instructional Components

Learning Opportunities

Three unique opportunities to engage students built into the daily schedule

Learning Labs

  • 6-hour, immersive learning environment solving a real-world challenge through the lens of construction and maritime with culinary added in year 2.
  • 8:1 student-to-instructor ratio

Block Classes

  • 110-minute class periods in a designated subject, naturally broken up by lunch, to allow for longer, more in depth project or activity completion.
  • 16:1 student-to-instructor ratio

Discussions

  • 55-minute periods with dynamic, hands-on classroom learning and discussion
  • 16:1 student-to-instructor ratio

Unique 11th and 12th grade opportunities

Internships (11th Grade)

  • Working internally with one of the SEUs to develop stronger workplace habits and skills in incubated environment

Apprenticeships (12th grade)

  • Externships facilitated by relationships developed by the SEUs
  • Real, on-the-job experience with little incubation
  • Commensurate compensation for the student’s time, effort, and jobsite maturity

Student Support

Daily Feedback

Students recieve daily feedback from their teachers on behavior

  • Professionalism Scores based on maturity, effort, and time management
  • Provides real-time feedback to parents to create a stronger partnerships
  • Sparates behavior and engagement from academic performance

Weekly Feedback

Students received weekly narrative feedback from each instructor

  • Details academic components such as knowledge/skill mastery, newly emerging mastery/skills, areas of continued growth
  • Provides opportunity to develop a deeper relationship between student and instructor
  • Focuses student personal evaluation on actionable objectives rather than subjective number or letter

Crews

  • Each student is placed in a crew with the same adult crew leader for the duration of their 4 years
  • Students travel through classes in their crews and conclude each day with a crew meeting (except lab days)

Designated 12th Grade transition Facilitiator

  • Oversees student transition to next step
  • Coordinates job/college placement
  • Responsible for meeting with each student to help map individualized tasks and needs.
  • Additional touchpoint for parents/guardians for student transition

School Cultural Impact

  • Identify and speak with a consistent, constant caring adult
  • Create a sense of place and belonging
  • Talk specifically about teamwork and of collective accountability
  • Facilitate school-wide competition and incentives
  • Provide leadership experience

Term Evaluation

  • Students receive narrative evaluation from their crew leader as well as from each instructor detailing the student’s presence each term

Students receive an updated skills profile

  • All grade-level faculty meets to discuss each student
  • Determine, based on term’s objectives, which skills a student has increased mastery

Parental Support

Parenting tool-kit

Parenting tool-kit

  • Phone locking system
  • Daily feedback regarding student behavior and engagement
  • Weekly narrative feedback about academic performance
  • Real-time communication with crew leader
  • Access to family counseling
  • Parent group activities (sailing, boating)
  • Meeting times that work with parent schedules
  • Individualized support, as needed

Faculty Support

Faculty Support Measures

  • Protected vacation time
  • Protected planning time prior to each term
  • Weekly check-ins
  • Cross-disciplinary classroom observations - 1/term
  • Feedback and coaching with narrative comments
  • Guided discussion during student reviews
  • Agenda-driven meetings
  • Standing opportunities to provide feedback
  • Professional progress meetings - formal and informal

Assessment Design

Benchmark Assessment: MAP

  • Utilized at the beginning (Term 1), middle (Term 3), and end of each year (End of Term 5)
  • Utilized to evaluate student content acquisition against national benchmarks and standards
  • Results analyzed in conjunction with internal data (daily, weekly, term) to assess individual student needs and instructional processes

Formative and Summative Assessments by class

  • Explicit conversation about evaluation rubric at prior to each assessment
  • Narrative evaluation shared with student
  • Quantitative data is collected on the backend by instructors - this is not shared with the students but is used to inform narrative feedback to each student.

Transcript & Certifications

  • Professionalism score and predictive index
  • Crew leader endorsement and student statement
  • Skills Graph and knowledge acquisition bars
  • Summary of classes
  • Certifications (industry-specific: culinary, construction, maritime)

Instructional Philosophy

Instructional Philosophy

SailFuture Academy is a private high school that prepares students to think critically and effectively solve problems in order to become business owners and operators.



Our instructional philosophy is rooted in the idea that learning by doing is the best way to engage and motivate learners who are disengaged in traditional school settings and modalities.

We believe that disengaged students are not at fault for poor academic performance. It is incumbent upon the school to design an educational model that shows students the value and purpose of the lessons and opportunities therein. Through dynamic lesson planning that is informed by the developmental needs of an adolescent, students learn by immediately synthesizing lesson content, critical thinking , and problem solving in order to demonstrate formative mastery throughout each term.

Each term has an anchor theme that guides the objectives and outcomes of a term across subjects. Therefore, the interconnectivity of the subjects is explicitly detailed thus helping out students see value in practical learning that relates to the immediate challenges they face in everyday life by learning through the why: why we do it, why we should know it, why it matters.
The SailFuture Academy’s academic design centers around the theme of entrepreneurship, integrating fundamental business concepts into the Humanities and STEM curriculums in all grades. Outside of the classroom, students participate in two 6-hour Learning Labs per week where they are exposed to real-world business problem solving challenges in the maritime, construction and culinary fields.

The SailFuture Academy’s academic design centers around the theme of entrepreneurship, integrating fundamental business concepts into the Humanities and STEM curriculums in all grades.



In the 11th and 12th grade years, students intern and apprentice in three student-led social enterprises - the SailFuture Kitchen, the SailFuture Construction Team, and the SailFuture Boat Donation program, with the option to specialize. In these years, students learn business by doing business while acquiring technical skills and building a resume that helps them prepare for career launch upon high school graduation.

We believe not only in the power of students but also in the power of meaningful relationships built on trust and respect. All of our students are grouped into crews of 8. Each crew will have its own name, identity, and crew leader. The crew system is an important part of helping each student feel seen, safe and secure in their learning space thereby facilitating a greater willingness to take risks and explore their interests without judgment.

Crews have one hour of dedicated meeting time each day during which crews discuss and work through important life lessons and social emotional skills in a more insulated environment. Additionally, crews traverse through the school day together and compete against each other in maritime, construction, and culinary projects, earning points based on their performance as a team. For example, the student body as a whole may vote on which crew prepared the best lunch each week or which crew designed the most interesting cardboard sailboat in their maritime lab.



Finally, our instructional philosophy is greatly impacted by our approach to student evaluation. All students will receive daily professionalism scores and weekly narrative evaluations on formative assessments from each instructor that provide meaningful feedback on each student’s strengths and weaknesses. At the conclusion of a term, students receive an updated skills profile detailing continued and/or new areas of strength, which is determined by the entire grade-level team.

This system focuses on the relationship between faculty members and students and makes the learning process more personal for both the instructor and the student while at the same time upholding and reinforcing the objectives of each term.
