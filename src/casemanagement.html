Case Management Program Overview

SailFuture operates two residential homes in St. Petersburg, Florida licensed by the Department of Children and Families. Each home accommodates 6 youth between the ages of 13 – 17.

In partnership with Eckerd Connects, Lutheran Services of Florida, and Medicaid, SailFuture provides dependency and targeted case management services to the highest-risk teens in foster care in Pinellas and Pasco counties.

Our Case Managers work hand in hand to help our high risk teens stabilize and navigate their way out of a complex foster care system and into a loving and permanent home.

Who we serve

On average our teens have had 10 or more foster care placements, attended 5 or more schools, and are unlikely to reunify with their families.

They have been referred to SailFuture for specialty case management services because existing providers have been unable to meet their unique needs and manage their challenging behaviors.

Our teens’ complex trauma leads to challenging behaviors. Our team focuses on interpreting and decoding those behaviors to understand the underlying message.

Age Range: 13 - 17 years old
Referral Source: Youth in foster care
Gender: Male and Female

Our Approach

We recognize that teenagers have different needs and behaviors than toddlers and infants. Traditional case management – home visits, judicial reviews, and referral – is not enough to meet their unique needs.

In addition to traditional responsibilities, our case managers act as the basis of a teen’s support system, providing wraparound services to ensure that they are not falling through the cracks of the foster care system.

1 : 10 caseloads allow our case managers to adopt a ‘whatever it takes philosophy, providing everything from sailing based interventions to advanced mental health and education support.

Sailing Based Interventions

Sailing-based interventions are a critical therapeutic and functional element of our dependency case management model.

Every week our case managers take their teens and families sailing to build rapport and work through experience-based challenges to develop trust, camaraderie, and teamwork.

Case Managers use the sailing platform to help our teens build relationships with their mental health counselors and potential caregivers. For many of our teens, sailing becomes a safe and healthy hobby – one that provides them with an opportunity to grow and heal on their own terms.
